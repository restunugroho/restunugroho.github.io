üéØ Problem

A major taxi company in Indonesia with hundreds of thousands customers and ten of thousands drivers faced a significant challenge during demand surges, such as heavy rain or major public events. During these periods, the order completion rate dropped sharply, causing customers to wait longer due to the unavailability of taxis. This led to a decline in customer trust. On the other side, many drivers were roaming without passengers, wasting both fuel and time. Drivers needed guidance on where to position themselves during idle periods to improve efficiency and earnings.

üéØ Objective

Design a system that recommends optimal standby locations for taxi drivers‚Äîbased on predicted passenger demand and existing taxi supply‚Äîso they can position themselves strategically when vacant.

  • Developed a demand forecasting model for each cluster by time of day and day of week to predict future passenger needs, enabling dynamic recommendations for high-probability pickup spots.
  • Integrated the forecasts into an optimization system that matches potential standby locations for each driver based on the demand‚Äìsupply balance.

üéØ Objective

  • Improved model performance through feature engineering (e.g., time of day, pickup‚Äìdrop-off location pairs, route variations) and model calibration.

3. Waiting Time Prediction for Taxi Drivers in Pangkalan

üéØ Focus Area IoT Analytics, Predictive Modeling
üìä Performance 80%+ Interval Coverage
üõ†Ô∏è Architecture Batch ML Pipeline
üíº Application Driver Mobile App
üéØ Problem

Pangkalan (taxi stands / waiting areas like in airports, malls, stations, hotels) have highly variable demand and supply. Some pangkalan suffer long passenger-arrival intervals while others have idle drivers waiting too long. Drivers lack a reliable, real-time way to decide which pangkalan to enter when they are vacant, causing wasted time, fuel, and lower earnings‚Äîand suboptimal customer service.

üéØ Objective

Predict the waiting time for each pangkalan (from the moment a driver enters until they get a passenger) and provide a min‚Äìmax prediction interval. These predictions are then used to recommend to drivers which pangkalan to enter‚Äîprioritizing those with the shortest expected waiting times. This helps drivers make data-driven decisions, improving match rates, reducing idle time, and increasing earnings.

üöÄ My Contribution
  • Calculated taxi waiting times by collecting and engineering IoT data at scale, leveraging BigQuery for processing
  • Designed waiting-time target and training dataset: Defined target as elapsed minutes between driver entry into pangkalan and next accepted pickup (with excluding for outlier data). Created features such as predicted demand, current number of taxi, and time series of the waiting time.
  • Developed prediction model that outputs waiting time and the interval min-max prediction.
  • Deployed end-to-end pipeline: Automated feature computation and prediction in Kubeflow, Integrated with driver app, Implemented monitoring dashboards for interval coverage, and prediction accuracy
üõ†Ô∏è Tech Stack

Data processing: Python and BigQuery; Modeling & experimentation: Python, pandas, scikit-learn, CatBoost; Orchestration & serving: Kubeflow; Monitoring & visualization: Looker Studio
